Environmental Voices that Inpire us.

These are some of the remarkable individuals that remind us what it to live in harmony with nature. They have shown love to the earth through science, storytelling, activism and everyday compassion.

Wangari Maathai

Remembering.(1940-2011)

Founder of The Green Belt Movement and the first african woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize, Wangari Maathai empowered communities across Kenya to plant and protect millions of trees, restore degraded lands and to protect their environment. She beliebved that caring for nature is inseprable from caring for people. A truth that remains in the heart of sustainability today.

"It's the litte things citizens do. That's what will make the difference. My little thing is planting trees.

Jane Goodall

Remembering.(1934-2025)

A pioneering primatologist and conservationist. Jane Goodall transformed our understanding of animals and their emotional depth. Though The Jane Goodall Institute, she advocated for wildlife protection, environmental education, and youth empowerment though her roots and shoots program.

David Attenborough

A legendary environmental broadcaster and natural historian, Sir David Attenborough has spent dacades educating us on the beauty and fragility of our planet through film and story telling. His documentaries have inspired millions to protect their environment and to see it, not as a separate being, but as who we are. Visit Our Planet to explore some of his films.

Greta Thunberg

Greta Thunberg is a Swedish environmental activist who's voice spoke alive a global movement. She reminded the world that change often starts with one courageous voice. She is an remarkable symbol of youth-led global activism.

"You are never too small to make a difference"
